Million Song Dataset (MSD), a famous dataset with freely available collection of audio features and metadata for a million contemporary popular music tracks.

There are many attractive features in the MSD. Here, what we want to focus are the year, location and genre of a song. Based on these features, for each year, we define the most prolific types of music over time and understand their evolution. The purpose of this project in Robert West's CS-401 Applied Data Analysis (ADA) class of Fall 2017 at EPFL is to show the evolution of the music genres over time and space.

A subset of 10.000 songs from the dataset we used for this project is avalaible for download here.



What are the main music genres ?

Since our general task is to model the genre of a the songs, we decided to classify every music under one of those genre :

  • Blues
  • Country
  • Electronic
  • Folk
  • Jazz
  • Pop
  • Metal
  • Rock
  • Orchestral
  • Hip-Hop
  • Raggae
  • World


Dataset description

The songs here are mostly ranging from 1920 to 2010, with a peak in the year 2000s.

The entire dataset is 280GB, so we used the cluster to work on it. The MSD contains some Additional files which provide us with all the different tags that could define a song.

We notice that the number of the songs starts to increase significantly in the 1980s, likely coinciding with the invention of the CD. CD sales began declining in 2000 due to the increasing popularity of a new digital format known as the MP3.
